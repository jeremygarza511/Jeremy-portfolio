Vision

Where I Am Headed

My goal is to build a career in software development or IT systems where I can solve real problems for real people. I started in customer-facing tech roles — Best Buy, IKEA, volunteer IT support — and those experiences showed me how much good software and reliable systems matter to people's daily lives. Now I am pursuing my degree at WGU and building the technical depth to work on those systems from the inside.

Short-Term Goals (1–2 Years)

Complete My BS in Software Engineering at WGU

My most immediate priority is finishing my degree. I am currently working through coursework in Advanced Java, back-end programming, data structures, and software security. Each course is building a foundation I plan to apply directly in a professional development role. I am pushing to accelerate my WGU term completion so I can enter the workforce sooner without sacrificing depth of understanding.

Land My First Technical Role

Whether that is a junior developer position, a help desk or IT support role, or a hybrid customer-technical position, I want to get my foot in the door of a team where I can grow. My experience with Salesforce CRM, Linux configuration, and hardware troubleshooting means I am not starting from zero — I just need the opportunity to apply those skills in a dedicated tech environment.

Earn a Relevant Certification

I am targeting either CompTIA A+ or the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner certification within the next year. Certifications validate the hands-on work I have been doing in my home lab and signal to employers that my skills are real, not just listed on a résumé.

Long-Term Goals (3–5 Years)

Grow Into a Full Software Developer Role

Long-term, I want to be writing production code as part of a development team — working on back-end systems, APIs, or internal tooling. My Java coursework and home lab experience have given me a genuine appreciation for how systems are structured. I want to deepen that understanding through real professional experience until I can contribute meaningfully to complex projects.
